The Story of This Blog, aka The Beginning

Let's talk about how this website came to be. I used to have another blog. It was really fancy, based on a Wordpress theme, and maintained by my dear friend Afshin. But, for so many reasons I didn't update it; one being a lack of planning for what the blog would be used for.

On February 28, 2026 a war broke out in the Middle East, between Iran and U.S.-Israel. I was leaving in Iran in that period. The Iranian government cut off international internet access. Internet was not only a source of entertainment, it was everything to us. So, I had a lot of freetime. I read books, watched movies, binge-watched series, listened to music, played some games, and planned for my future, but eventually, I got bored beyond my tolerance. Interestingly, there was a website in Iran that offered AI services. That was when the idea of a static website, a personal blog, came to my mind.

Why Windows 98?

Well, Windows 98 was my first experience with computers. Back in 2000 or 2001, one of my uncles won a competition or something like that and was awarded with a computer. That computer had a Lion King game. I went crazy over it. I loved the technology. My mother was trying to get her ICDL and she needed a computer. Yes, ICDL was a big thing back then. A few months later, my parents bought a PC. I just loved it. I loved playing games on it. And with every upgrade, it was even more interesting. It quickly became a gadget for playing games and watching movies.

I guess after a few years, we got an internet connection. It was like flying on top of the skies! There's no word to describe the feeling I had the first time I talked with a random stranger on Yahoo Messenger. I was literally bewildered. There were people coming over to just see how this internet thing was working. And everyone was shocked when they saw it for the first time. And I should say we only used it to chat with random strangers on Yahoo Messenger, because there was almost no Persian content online, and even if there was, I had no idea how to consume and use that content.

This website is kind of a nostalgic reminder to cherish those great days. Windows 98 was terrible; at least to us who used RTL Persian. We had to do many modifications and install a ton of things to make it useable with Persian fonts and Persian language overall. But it was fun. Installing Persian language modules felt like handling cutting-edge tech. I felt proud. I should've been proud; not bad for a 9- or 10-year-old boy installing Windows on new machines and handling complex stuff like setting up printers! Those were the days.

One other reason for designing the website like this, was that it was free! Yes, I'm serving it on GitHub Pages and at any moment in time they can stop serving it, but it's free. And even if I have to rent my own server to serve it, it wouldn't be a expensive since this is a rather light-weight static website. Plus that, this design system - if it can be called one! - is very simple and elegant and easy to maintain, and naturally encourages people to come and explore and read.
