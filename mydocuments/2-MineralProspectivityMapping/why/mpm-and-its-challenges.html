When MPM Becomes a Decision Marathon

I like this project very much. In fact, it has grown on me; since I actually didn't like it when I started working on it. At first everything was very vague and I didn't know what I was doing. I guess the problem was the fact that the online machine learning course I took, was not really teaching me things I needed in handling a mineral prospectivity mapping project. It was good, but for handling business related problems. I soon learned, painfully, that mineral prospectivity mapping requires a different set of machine learning skills and tool set.

After a few days and running the pipeline 4 times end to end, I decided that the baseline was fine and I needed to move on to the next step; which was feature selection. Feature selection and feature engineering for MPM is really challenging. There are several reasons as to why. First, number of engineered features can increase exponentially because there are a lot of useful information within each evidential layer. Second, there are several different methods for feature selection and they require a great amount of computational power. Doing that on a 2020 MacBook Air is not a really wise thing. Yet, I do that nonetheless, since I don't have any other choice. And finally, I'm again at that moment in the project I feel overwhelmed by the shear number of decisions I need to make so the model can work and that makes me very exhaused.

A Pile of Decisions

You see, we are at an age that doing things means making the right decisions - at least in tech. Vibe-coding has become the norm and the art of coding has become mundane. You just need to make the right decisions and choose the best outcome and off you go with a new fully-functioning website in a few hours at most! The bottleneck has shifted from coding skills to prompt engineering and decision making skills. That is kind of cool, but it has its own caveats.

I like doing things. I mean when I'm told, or when I know, what to do, I go on and do that thing. I mean when the path is clear, you have a mental model of what can be done, you can easily go on and do it. You don't think about it. It's like learning to drive a car; at first it's a great challenge and you don't even have neuromuscular coordination. After a while, you'd be crusing at 80 km/h while texting your friend, when you're on a call with your boss, and enjoying your morining coffee all at the same time! I don't approve of this, of course. I just want to say it becomes easy after a while, whe you get the hang of doing that thing. But making decisions never gets easy.

As a person who likes making programs, vibe-coding has turned making an app from a physical experience of coding, to more of a decision making process. You just need to make the right decisions. And even if you make the wrong decisions, git is there to help you. That decision making process takes a lot of energy. Like way too much. And with AI coding assistants helping you, you tend to make even more decisions than you need and over-engineer an idea. Decision making, on a scale of more than 100 or so about a single subject in a few hours, overwhelms me a lot. I don't know that's the case for you too or not.

I have realized that when I feel overwhelmed, like I don't know what I'm doing, I'm just doing something, that makes me not like the thing I'm doing. That's basically why it took me too much time to do my master's thesis; because I didn't know how to do it, and I didn't even know the problem at first. How can you solve something that you don't know, and you don't even have the skill to solve it?! Isn't that absurd? Yes, I chose my research topic, but I wasn't fully aware of the details, nor did I have the skill set for solving the problems. Right now, I feel exactly like back then. Hopefully, AI is here to save the day. AI can help a lot with doing preliminary research. It also helps with deep research, but that also needs human skills as you have to have an analytical mind to help with asking the right questions.

Back to MPM, It's at a stage that I need to make too manu decisions again. Currently I'm working on feature selection and feature engineering. One thing that bothers me is that I don't know what the next step can be. That's a separate misery that I'll tend to once this stage is over. I'm just very curious to turn this project into an app so others can use and enjoy.
